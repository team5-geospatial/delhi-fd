Fractal Dimension Analysis

Analysis Section

The fractal image of Delhi's urban structure reveals a high-complexity growth pattern with a likely fractal dimension value between 1.6-1.9, indicating intense, irregular expansion. The dense, interconnected patterns reflect Delhi's radial-concentric development around historical cores (Old Delhi, Connaught Place) merging with chaotic suburban sprawl (Noida, Gurgaon). The texture shows strong linear corridors along metro/road networks (e.g., NH24, Ring Roads) with fragmented clusters in peripheral zones, highlighting both planned infrastructure and unregulated growth. This fractal complexity underscores Delhi's challenge in balancing organic expansion with sustainable planning, particularly in managing the urban-rural fringe where development appears most disordered.
